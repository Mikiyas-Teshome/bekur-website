## Brand positioning

### The problem we own

Small and lower mid-market **professional service firms** carry **AI hype fatigue**. They have been burned by tools that promised transformation and delivered chaos, or by generic AI wrappers that broke on real-world data.

Five pains repeat across our market:

1. **Rigid SaaS & template fighting** — Bending proprietary workflows to fit vertical tools (Clio, Harvey, TaxDome, HoneyBook, etc.). The workflow is the asset; the tool should adapt.
2. **Opaque & skyrocketing AI costs** — Token and API bills that scale faster than value, with no architectural discipline or caps.
3. **Fragmented data silos** — Automation in one box while real work lives in email, spreadsheets, CRM, and legacy drives.
4. **Hype projects that fail** — Long cycles, unclear ROI, quietly shelved — budget, appetite, and team trust all gone.
5. **Billable hour leakage** — Partners and fee-earners losing hundreds of hours a year to intake, document chase, invoicing reconciliation, and admin bloat.

### Our position in the market

**Full positioning statement:**

> For small professional service firms suffering from AI hype fatigue — burned by rigid SaaS and generic AI wrappers that break on real-world data — Bekur Technologies is the logic-first antidote. We map how the firm actually operates, then ship custom automation that adapts to their stack, with hard monthly spend caps, 3–6 week delivery, and measurable ROI within 90 days.

**Plain English:**

We don't bend your firm to a tool. We map how you actually work, then ship one workflow that fits — in weeks, for a fixed price, with partner approval on every client-facing step and a hard cap on AI cost.

### Category & offer

| Layer | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| **What we are** | An operational systems firm — not a generic AI agency, not a SaaS vendor, not an enterprise consultancy. |
| **What we sell** | Recovered partner capacity, protected margins, and predictable delivery — not features or “digital transformation.” |
| **How we deliver** | **The Automation Sprint:** Logic Mapping → fixed-scope build → approval & go-live. |
| **Commercial shape** | Paid **Blueprint** phase optional front door; sprint engagements typically **$4,500–$26,000** by offer and tier (Pilot / Standard / Mid-Market). |

### What we do / what we don't do

**We do:**

- Map the firm's business logic **before** writing code.
- Ship **one production workflow** per Automation Sprint (3–6 weeks, fixed scope, fixed price).
- Build **into** the existing stack (email, drives, CRM, spreadsheets) instead of replacing it.
- Set **hard monthly AI spend caps** in scope before kickoff.
- Engineer for **measurable ROI within 90 days** of go-live.

**We don't:**

- Bill open-ended hours or run discovery that drifts for months.
- Take multiple workflows in one sprint when asked to “do everything.”
- Use juniors learning on client projects.
- Promise compliance certifications (SOC 2, HIPAA pipelines) we cannot credibly deliver.
- Pitch generic AI wrappers we would not trust on real-world firm data.

## Ideal customer profile (ICP)

### Who we serve (primary)

**Decision-maker:** Founder or managing partner of a **5–50 person professional service firm**.

**Geography:** English-speaking markets — **US, UK, Canada, Australia** (global delivery from Addis Ababa; firm voice is operator-first, not geography-led).

**Revenue band:** Approximately **$500K–$5M** per year.

**Psychographic:** Smart enough to know they need **custom, not median SaaS** — but have not found a partner they trust to map logic and ship without scope creep.

> **Important:** Industries below are **illustrative**. The wedge is the **operator profile + pain pattern** (AI hype fatigue, manual admin, stack fragmentation), not a single vertical lock-in. We test across professional services; messaging stays pain-led, not “law-only” or “accounting-only.”

### Industries (illustrative, not exclusive)

Law · accounting · management consulting · financial advisory · recruitment · architecture · real estate brokerage · marketing and professional agencies · similar boutique knowledge-work firms.

### Firmographics & technographics

| Attribute | Typical pattern |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Team size** | 5–50 staff |
| **Tools** | 4–8 disconnected SaaS tools that do not talk to each other |
| **Time leak** | 10–20 hours/week on admin that “should be automated” |
| **History** | Tried off-the-shelf tools; frustrated by template fit |
| **Buying** | Founder or managing partner decides — **not** IT, procurement, or innovation committee |

### Operator profile

- Often **40–55**, second-time founder or **10+ years** in practice.
- **The** decision-maker — would not delegate this to an IT lead for a first engagement.
- Cares about **billable hours, margin, and team adoption** — not architecture for its own sake.

### What they say (use in messaging & discovery)

- “We keep doing this manually because nothing fits how we work.”
- “I looked at [tool] but it would force us to change too much.”
- “I want to use AI but I don't know where to start — and I don't trust most people pitching me.”
- “I need someone who understands our business, not just the code.”
- “The last developer was great at code and useless at understanding what we actually do.”

### What they fear

- Another developer who burns budget without shipping anything the team uses.
- A tool that locks them into someone else's workflow.
- An AI “science experiment” that does not move revenue.
- Being sold by someone who does not understand how a practice actually runs.
- Their team rejecting another system they did not ask for.

### What they want

- **One workflow** off their plate, permanently.
- A partner who understands the **business**, not only the stack.
- **Predictable** cost, timeline, and scope.
- Something the team will use **the next morning**.
- **More partner-level hours** back in the week.

### Where they are reachable

- **LinkedIn** (primary) — industry operators, not tech influencers.
- Niche communities (bar associations, AICPA, MCA, ICAEW, founder Slack groups).
- Substacks and newsletters by senior operators in their field.
- Podcasts about **running a practice**, not scaling startups.
- Regional industry conferences (roughly 200–800 attendees).
- Rarely Twitter/X for business; almost never TikTok or Reddit for this buying motion.

Score prospects before booking discovery. **Below 60** → polite decline. **60–79** → book with caution. **80+** → high-fit.

| Professional service firm | 15 | Law, accounting, consulting, advisory, agency, recruitment, real estate, etc. |
| Firm size 5–50 | 10 | LinkedIn / website |
| Revenue ~$500K–$5M | 10 | Inferred from team size, positioning |
| Decision-maker in thread | 20 | Founder, managing partner, COO — not IT-only |
| Named active pain | 15 | Matches one of the five pains above |
| Budget signal | 10 | Spend on tools/contractors $5K+/quarter acknowledged |

**Automatic disqualifiers:** SOC 2/HIPAA demands we cannot meet · full SaaS replacement (not one workflow) · decision-by-committee · refuses paid Blueprint / logic mapping · “fast and cheap everything” with no trade-off acknowledgment.

## What makes Bekur different

### One-sentence difference

**We don't bend your firm to the tool. We build the tool to your firm.**

### vs. the four alternatives

| Alternative | Where they fail | Where Bekur wins |
|-------------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Vertical SaaS** (Clio, Harvey, TaxDome, HoneyBook, Karbon, etc.) | Built for the median firm; painful when logic deviates; you bend to the product | We map **your** logic and build around **your** stack |
| **Generic AI agencies** | Open-ended hours, weak domain context, demos not production; uncapped AI cost | Fixed scope, fixed price, **hard monthly spend cap**, production workflow |
| **Freelance developers** | Build what you describe; rarely challenge broken business logic | **Logic Mapping is a deliverable** before code |
| **Enterprise consultancies** | Procurement, six-figure floors, 18-month roadmaps | **One sprint at a time**, ROI inside **90 days**, no procurement theater |

### Five brand values (how difference shows up)

1. **Logic before code** — Mapping and partner sign-off before implementation.
2. **Accountability by design** — Fixed scope, fixed price, written AI cap, approval gates, change orders only.
3. **Proof over promises** — Credibility from shipped systems (e.g. production apps at scale), not pitch decks.
4. **Small is intentional** — Senior-only team; the people in the room build the work.
5. **Built for operators** — Managing partners and founders, not IT departments or innovation committees.

### Delivery promises (non-negotiables)

| Promise | Commitment |
|---------|------------|
| **Time to live** | 3–6 weeks per Automation Sprint (one workflow) |
| **Economics** | Hard monthly AI spend cap in scope before kickoff |
| **ROI window** | Measurable outcome target within **90 days** of go-live |
| **Risk gate** | Paid **Blueprint** ($2,500 tier) maps logic and fixes sprint quote before full build |

## Voice guardrails (for all external copy)

**Sound like Bekur:**

- “We start by mapping how your firm actually works — before we write a line of code.”
- “One Automation Sprint. One workflow. Hard monthly spend cap, written into the scope.”
- “We don't sell AI. We end AI hype fatigue.”

**Do not sound like Bekur:**

- “We leverage cutting-edge AI solutions to optimize operational efficiency.”
- “Our team delivers world-class digital transformation.”
- “Innovative. Agile. Future-ready.”

**Principles:** Lead with their problem · use specific proof · business language first · short sentences · honest about limits.
