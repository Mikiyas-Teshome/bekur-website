The real cost of manual operations

Your firm has the right people.
It's running on the wrong systems.

Most professional service firms have invested in tools. The problem isn't the tools — it's the operational gaps between them. These are the six patterns we see in every firm we audit.

⚡

No single source of truth for active work

Matter status, project ownership, and deadlines live in five different places. Leadership can't see what's happening without asking five people. Decisions get made on gut feel.

📋

Client onboarding takes two weeks and drops tasks

New client setup is a 12-step email-and-spreadsheet process. Every person does it differently. First impressions suffer. Senior staff spend hours on work that should take minutes.

⏰

Compliance deadlines live in individual heads

Regulatory dates sit in shared calendars and personal notebooks. One missed filing creates liability multiples larger than any system investment. The risk is real and it compounds.

📂

Staff spend 5–8 hours a week chasing documents

Every engagement involves cycles of follow-up emails for signatures, forms, and files. It's demoralizing. It delays delivery. And the ROI of fixing it is calculated in days, not months.

📊

Management reports take two days to build manually

Partners and ops staff pull data from billing, PM tools, and spreadsheets every week. By Monday, the report is already stale. Leadership is flying blind on decisions that cost real money.

🔒

Critical knowledge is locked in your best people

When a senior person is out or leaves, the firm doesn't know how to handle their accounts. Key-person risk isn't just an HR issue — it's an operational one. It's a solved problem. Most firms just haven't solved it yet.

We already have software. Why do we need this?
Software doesn't build operational systems — it gives you tools to build them yourself. Firms that buy Clio, Karbon, and Xero and expect the operational problems to go away find that the problems follow them into the new software. We build the system around your actual workflows and connect your existing tools. The question isn't whether you have tools. It's whether your operations actually work.
What does this actually cost, relative to what we'd save?
A 20-person firm where senior staff spend 15% of their week on admin is losing roughly $468,000 in annual billing capacity. Our typical engagement — fully loaded, including retainer for year one — is $25,000–$40,000. At your billing rates, recovering 6 hours per week per fee-earner pays for the engagement within a quarter. We will run this calculation for your firm in the audit, with your numbers.
